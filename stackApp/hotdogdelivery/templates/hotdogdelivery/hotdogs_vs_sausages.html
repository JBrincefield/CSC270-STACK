🌭 Hotdogs vs Sausages 🌭

NO, hotdogs are NOT the same as sausages, and I'm sick of hearing this brain-dead argument from people who apparently think every tube-shaped meat is automatically identical. That's like saying a Lamborghini and a lawn mower are the same because they both have engines. Sausages are greasy, over-seasoned identity crises stuffed into a casing by people desperate to feel cultured. Every bite is a gamble between "too salty," "too chewy," or "why does this taste like burnt herbs and sadness?"

Hotdogs don't need to hide behind fake sophistication because hotdogs already achieved perfection. Clean flavor. Perfect texture. Reliable. A hotdog knows its purpose: dominate cookouts, baseball games, street carts, and midnight cravings without turning into some pretentious culinary lecture. You put a hotdog in a bun with mustard and suddenly life makes sense again. Meanwhile sausages need a five-minute explanation before anyone even wants to eat them.

And STOP saying "well technically hotdogs are sausages." Technically, a shark and a goldfish are both fish too, but if you can't tell the difference, you should not be trusted near a kitchen. Hotdogs are superior in every possible way, and the fact people still compare them to sausages makes me feel like I'm trapped in a civilization-ending experiment run by idiots.
